Your Journey Plan

Guwahati → Shillong → Cherrapunji → Dawki → Jowai → Guwahati

Day 01

Guwahati → Shillong | Arrival · Lakeside · City Evening

  • Pickup from Guwahati Airport / Railway Station — Begin your Meghalaya journey
  • Umiam Lake (Barapani) — Scenic en route stop; serene lakeside views and misty hills
  • Ward's Lake — Charming colonial-era garden lake at the heart of Shillong
  • Police Bazaar — Shillong's bustling market hub; street food, shopping and evening city vibe
  • Arrive Shillong — Check-in and overnight rest
🏨 Stay — Shillong
💡 Tip: Guwahati to Shillong is approx 3 hrs. Umiam Lake is a great pitstop to stretch. Police Bazaar is liveliest after 5 PM — perfect for a relaxed evening walk.
Day 02

Nongjrong Sunrise → Laitlum Canyon → Cherrapunji

  • Nongjrong Sunrise Point — Breathtaking panoramic sunrise over the Meghalaya plateau; arrive before dawn
  • Laitlum Canyon — 'End of Hills' in Khasi; dramatic canyon gorge with sweeping valley views
  • Shillong Peak — Highest point in Meghalaya at 6,449 ft; panoramic city and hill views (ID proof mandatory)
  • Elephant Falls — Beautiful 3-tiered waterfall just outside Shillong; lush forested setting
  • Transfer to Cherrapunji — Scenic drive through East Khasi Hills
🏨 Stay — Cherrapunji
💡 Tip: Start as early as 5 AM for Nongjrong sunrise. Laitlum is best in the morning before clouds roll in. ID proof is mandatory for Shillong Peak — carry original.
Day 03

Cherrapunji Local Sightseeing | Waterfalls · Caves · Classic Sohra Circuit

  • Seven Sisters Falls — Iconic 7-segmented seasonal waterfall; most spectacular during and after monsoon
  • Mawsmai Cave — Illuminated limestone cave with natural formations; easy walk-through suitable for all
  • Nohkalikai Falls — India's tallest plunge waterfall at 340m; best viewed in morning light
  • Wei Sawdong Falls — Hidden 3-tier waterfall with emerald pools; steep descent involved (wear grip footwear)
🏨 Stay — Cherrapunji
💡 Tip: Pack this day carefully — 4 major spots. Start by 8 AM sharp. Wei Sawdong descent is steep; wear grip footwear. Seven Sisters Falls is seasonal and best Jun–Oct.
Day 04

Mawlynnong → Dawki River Activities | Cleanest Village · River Fun

  • Mawlynnong Village — Asia's cleanest village; immaculate lanes, bamboo dustbins and community pride
  • Balancing Rock — Natural wonder; a giant boulder perfectly balanced on a smaller rock
  • Single Decker Living Root Bridge — A living bridge grown from tree roots over centuries; unique bio-engineering
  • Dawki River Boating — Crystal-clear Umngot River; the riverbed is visible through the water
  • Cliff Jumping at Dawki / Shnongpdeng — Thrilling cliff jump into the emerald river (optional)
🏨 Stay — Dawki / Shnongpdeng
💡 Tip: Mawlynnong is closed on Sundays — plan accordingly. Carry a change of clothes for river activities at Dawki. Don't miss sunset at Shnongpdeng.
Day 05

Krem Chympe Cave → Khaddum Waterfall → Jowai | Cave Adventure · Jaintia Hills

  • Krem Chympe Cave — Raw unlit cave adventure with Jeep Ride included; one of the most thrilling cave experiences in Meghalaya
  • Khaddum Waterfall — Beautiful hidden waterfall tucked in the Jaintia Hills; serene and relatively untouched
  • Drive to Jowai — Scenic transfer through East Meghalaya highlands
  • Arrive Jowai — Check-in and evening local exploration
🏨 Stay — Jowai
💡 Tip: Krem Chympe is a raw unlit cave — carry a torch. Not recommended for claustrophobic guests. Start early to reach Jowai comfortably.
Day 06

Jowai → Guwahati | Departure Day

  • Check-out from Jowai — Post-breakfast departure
  • Scenic drive back through the Meghalaya highlands
  • Drop at Guwahati Airport / Railway Station — Tour ends here with beautiful memories
✈️ Departure — Guwahati
💡 Tip: Start by 7–8 AM from Jowai for a comfortable drive to Guwahati (~4 hrs). Allow extra buffer time during peak season.

Before You Go

Travel Tips & Essentials

🌤️ Best Time
October–May for clear skies and waterfalls. Monsoon (Jun–Sep) makes roads risky but Seven Sisters Falls is at its best.
🏘️ Mawlynnong
Asia's cleanest village is closed on Sundays — plan Day 4 accordingly.
🕳️ Krem Chympe
Raw unlit cave — torch is mandatory. Jeep Ride is arranged. Not recommended for claustrophobic guests.
🚣 Dawki River
Boating and river activities at Dawki / Shnongpdeng at extra cost. Carry a change of clothes.
📋 Permits
Shillong Peak requires valid Govt. ID proof — carry original, not photocopy.
📶 Connectivity
Mobile signal can be weak in Jowai, Dawki and Jaintia Hills. Download offline maps before departure.
🎒 Gear
Comfortable grip footwear for caves and Wei Sawdong descent. Light rain jacket recommended year-round.
