Your Journey Plan

Guwahati → Sangti Valley → Dirang → Tawang → Bomdila → Guwahati

Day 01

Guwahati → Sangti Valley | Arrival · Himalayan Foothills (~320 km / 8–9 hrs)

  • Pickup from Guwahati Airport / Railway Station
  • Maa Kamakhya Temple — sacred Shakti Peetha; hilltop temple with panoramic views (en route)
  • Bhalukpong — Arunachal Pradesh entry formalities & ILP check
  • Kameng River Scenic Drive — stunning gorge drive along the turquoise Kameng
  • Tipi Orchidarium — one of India's largest orchid research centres (optional photo stop)
  • Arrive Sangti Valley — evening riverside walk & village exploration
🏨 Stay — Sangti Valley
💡 Tip: Long driving day — carry snacks and water. ILP (Inner Line Permit) is mandatory for Arunachal Pradesh — arrange in advance.
Day 02

Sangti → Dirang → Tawang | High Altitude Mountain Drive (~150 km / 7–8 hrs)

  • Dirang Monastery — ancient monastery perched above the Dirang Valley
  • Dirang Dzong — traditional fortified village with centuries-old history (optional)
  • Sela Pass (13,700 ft) — one of the world's highest motorable passes; snow-capped year round
  • Sela Lake — glacial lake at the top of Sela Pass; hauntingly beautiful
  • Jaswant Garh War Memorial — tribute to the 1962 Indo-China war hero
  • Nuranang (Jang) Waterfall — dramatic 100m waterfall cascading through the cliffs
  • Arrive Tawang
🏨 Stay — Tawang
💡 Tip: Sela Pass can close due to snow or bad weather — have a contingency plan. Altitude sickness possible above 12,000 ft — acclimatize slowly.
Day 03

Tawang Local Sightseeing | Monasteries, Memorials & Markets

  • Tawang Monastery — largest monastery in India; 400-year-old Tibetan Buddhist marvel
  • Urgelling Monastery — birthplace of the 6th Dalai Lama
  • Tawang War Memorial — solemn tribute to soldiers of the 1962 war
  • Giant Buddha Statue Viewpoint — massive golden Buddha overlooking the Tawang valley
  • Local Craft Centre & Market — Tibetan handicrafts, thangkas, woollen goods
🏨 Stay — Tawang
💡 Tip: Tawang Monastery is best visited in the morning when monks are active. Dress modestly at all monasteries. Carry cash — ATMs can be unreliable.
Day 04

Bum La Pass Excursion | High Altitude Border Excursion (Permit Required)

  • Bum La Pass (15,200 ft) — India-China border pass; one of the highest motorable points in Arunachal
  • Madhuri Lake (Sangestar Tso) — stunning glacial lake made famous by a Bollywood song
  • PTSO Lake — serene high-altitude lake surrounded by snow peaks
  • Return to Tawang by evening
🏨 Stay — Tawang
💡 Tip: Bum La Pass requires a special permit — arrange through your hotel/local agent in Tawang. Only Indian nationals allowed. Not accessible during heavy snowfall.
Day 05

Tawang → Bomdila | Mountain Drive & Local Sightseeing (~170 km / 6–7 hrs)

  • Sela Pass — photo stop if weather permits; different light, equally dramatic
  • Mountain & valley viewpoints — multiple scenic stops along the descent
  • Bomdila Monastery — beautiful monastery with sweeping Himalayan panoramas
  • Sunset Viewpoint — golden hour views over the West Kameng valley
  • Bomdila Local Market — local handicrafts and Tibetan artefacts
🏨 Stay — Bomdila
💡 Tip: Start early for a comfortable drive. Bomdila is a pleasant and relaxed town — good for an easy evening stroll.
Day 06

Bomdila → Guwahati | Departure Day (~330 km / 7–8 hrs)

  • After breakfast, check out and begin drive to Guwahati
  • Bhalukpong Exit — Arunachal Pradesh border checkpoint
  • Scenic foothills drive through Assam tea country
  • Drop at Guwahati Airport / Railway Station
✈️ Departure — Guwahati
💡 Tip: Start by 7 AM for a comfortable drive. Factor in border checkpoint time at Bhalukpong. Do not cut departure time too close.

Before You Go

Travel Tips & Essentials

🌤️ Best Time
March–June & September–November. Bum La Pass open only May–October. Avoid monsoon for road safety.
📋 Permits
ILP mandatory for all non-Arunachal residents. Bum La Pass needs additional special permit — arrange in Tawang.
🏔️ Altitude
Sela Pass (13,700 ft) and Bum La (15,200 ft) — altitude sickness possible. Acclimatize, stay hydrated, avoid alcohol.
📶 Connectivity
Very limited network in Tawang and Sangti. Download offline maps. Carry a power bank.
🧥 Gear
Heavy woolens mandatory even in summer. Sunscreen, sunglasses, lip balm for high altitude. Waterproof jacket recommended.
💵 Cash
Carry sufficient cash from Guwahati. ATMs in Tawang are unreliable and often out of service.
