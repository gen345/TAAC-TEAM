Your Journey Plan

Guwahati → Kaziranga → Nameri/Bhalukpong → Sangti Valley → Tawang → Shergaon → Guwahati

Day 01

Guwahati → Kaziranga | Wildlife · Jeep Safari · Assam Countryside (~220 km / 5–6 hrs)

  • Pickup from Guwahati Airport / Railway Station
  • Scenic drive through Assam countryside & iconic tea garden landscapes
  • Arrive Kaziranga National Park — check-in
  • Afternoon Jeep Safari — Central or Western Range; spot the Great One-Horned Rhinoceros, wild elephants, buffalo & deer
  • Orchid & Biodiversity Park — explore Assam's rich floral diversity
🏨 Stay — Kaziranga
💡 Tip: Book Jeep Safari slots in advance — they fill up fast. The Central Range is best for rhinos; Western Range offers open grassland views. Carry binoculars.
Day 02

Kaziranga → Nameri / Bhalukpong | Elephant Safari · Forest · Riverside (~150 km / 4–5 hrs)

  • Early Morning Elephant Safari at Kaziranga — dawn ride through tall elephant grass; closest approach to rhinos in the wild
  • Post breakfast check-out and transfer to Nameri National Park / Bhalukpong
  • En route scenic forest drives through the Assam–Arunachal foothills
  • Check-in at Nameri / Bhalukpong
  • Evening Riverside Walk near Jia Bhoroli River — crystal-clear river, birdwatching & peaceful surroundings
🏨 Stay — Nameri / Bhalukpong
💡 Tip: Elephant Safari must be pre-booked; slots are limited and allocated by the forest department. Nameri is excellent for birdwatching. The Jia Bhoroli is ideal for evening photography.
Day 03

Nameri → Dirang → Sangti Valley | Arunachal Entry · Monastery · Himalayan Valley (~180 km / 6–7 hrs)

  • After breakfast, depart for Sangti Valley via Dirang
  • Bhalukpong ILP Check — Arunachal Pradesh Inner Line Permit entry formalities
  • Scenic Kameng Valley Drive — dense forests and river gorges through the Kameng corridor
  • Dirang Monastery — serene ancient Buddhist monastery overlooking the valley
  • Dirang Dzong — traditional fortified village with centuries-old architecture (optional)
  • Arrive Sangti Valley — evening riverside walk & village exploration
🏨 Stay — Sangti Valley
💡 Tip: Keep Inner Line Permits ready at Bhalukpong — entry checks can take time. Sangti Valley is a hidden gem. Look out for Black-necked Cranes (Oct–Mar).
Day 04

Sangti Valley → Tawang | High Altitude Passes · Waterfalls · War Memorial (~150 km / 7–8 hrs)

  • After breakfast, depart for Tawang
  • Sela Pass (13,700 ft) — breathtaking high-altitude pass; snow-capped peaks and glacial lake views
  • Sela Lake — sacred glacial lake beside the pass; serene and often frozen in winter
  • Jaswant Garh War Memorial — tribute to Rifleman Jaswant Singh Rawat, hero of the 1962 war
  • Nuranang (Jang) Waterfall — thundering 100m waterfall; one of Northeast India's most spectacular sights
  • Arrive Tawang — check-in & rest
🏨 Stay — Tawang
💡 Tip: Sela Pass can be cold and icy — carry warm layers regardless of season. Start by 7 AM. Nuranang involves a short descent — wear grip footwear.
Day 05

Tawang Local Sightseeing | Monasteries · Memorials · Culture · Market

  • Tawang Monastery — Asia's largest Buddhist monastery at 10,000 ft; 17th-century masterpiece housing 500+ monks
  • Urgelling Monastery — birthplace of the 6th Dalai Lama; one of Arunachal's most revered sacred sites
  • Tawang War Memorial — poignant memorial with light & sound show
  • Giant Buddha Statue Viewpoint — panoramic views of Tawang valley and snow peaks
  • Local Craft Centre & Market — Monpa handloom, thangkas, traditional masks, local cheese & butter tea
🏨 Stay — Tawang
💡 Tip: Tawang Monastery is best visited early morning before tour groups arrive. Carry ID proof for war memorial entry. The local market is the best place for authentic Monpa handicrafts.
Day 06

Bum La Pass Excursion | Indo-China Border · Alpine Lakes (Army Permit Required)

  • Bum La Pass (~15,200 ft) — Indo-China border point; special Army permit required
  • Madhuri Lake (Sangestar Tso) — stunning high-altitude lake surrounded by snow peaks; filming location of Koyla
  • PTSO Lake — pristine alpine lake en route to Bum La; peaceful & less visited
  • Y-Junction Viewpoint — strategic and scenic military vantage point
  • Return to Tawang / Nuranang by evening
🏨 Stay — Tawang / Nuranang
💡 Tip: Bum La requires a special Army permit — arrange through a registered Tawang agency at least a day prior. Subject to Army & weather clearance. Not advised for those with altitude sickness.
Day 07

Tawang → Shergaon | Scenic Transfer · Monastery · Offbeat Village Stay (~170 km / 6–7 hrs)

  • After breakfast, transfer towards Shergaon
  • Sela Pass Photo Stop — last crossing of the iconic pass (weather permitting)
  • Mountain & valley viewpoints — sweeping panoramas of the Eastern Himalayas en route
  • Bomdila Monastery — peaceful gompa with beautiful valley views over the Kameng corridor
  • Shergaon Village Walk — explore traditional Sherdukpen homestays and community life
  • Monastery & forest surroundings — ancient monastery set amidst dense oak and rhododendron forests
🏨 Stay — Shergaon
💡 Tip: Shergaon is highly recommended for travellers seeking something offbeat and authentic — quieter, more scenic and a genuine village experience.
Day 08

Shergaon → Guwahati | Departure Day (~300–340 km / 7–8 hrs)

  • After breakfast, check-out and transfer to Guwahati
  • Scenic drive through the Assam foothills and tea gardens
  • Optional stop at Bhalukpong for river views & refreshments (if time permits)
  • Drop at Guwahati Airport / Railway Station
✈️ Departure — Guwahati
💡 Tip: Start by 7 AM for a comfortable drive. Road conditions between Shergaon and Guwahati can vary. Do not plan a tight connection on this day.

Before You Go

Travel Tips & Essentials

🗓️ Best Time
October–April. Avoid monsoon (Jun–Sep) — Kaziranga floods and Arunachal roads see heavy landslides. Winter offers snow but Sela Pass may temporarily close.
📋 Permits
ILP mandatory for all non-Arunachal residents. Bum La Pass needs an additional Army permit — arrange in Tawang a day prior.
🦏 Kaziranga Safaris
Book Jeep & Elephant Safaris in advance. Early morning slots give the best wildlife sightings. Safaris run at dawn and afternoon.
🏔️ Altitude
Tawang: ~10,000 ft · Sela Pass: 13,700 ft · Bum La: ~15,200 ft. Take it slow on Day 4. Avoid alcohol on first night in Tawang.
📶 Connectivity
Mobile signal weak beyond Dirang. BSNL works best in Arunachal. Download offline maps. Carry power banks — charging options limited in remote areas.
🎒 Gear
Warm layers essential even Oct–Apr (thermals, jacket, gloves, woollen cap). Grip footwear for waterfalls. Sunscreen & lip balm for high-altitude UV.
