Your Journey Plan

Guwahati to Guwahati | 2N Shillong + 2N Cherrapunji + 1N Dawki

Day 01

Guwahati → Shillong | Arrival & City Exploration

  • Pickup from Guwahati Airport (by 8 AM)
  • Umiam Lake (Barapani) — Scenic stop en route; optional lakeside stroll
  • Don Bosco Museum — Remarkable 7-floor museum of Northeast India's cultures
  • Golf Course — One of the oldest and most scenic golf courses in Asia
  • Ward's Lake — Charming colonial-era lake in the heart of Shillong
🏨 Stay — Shillong
💡 Tip: Arrive Guwahati Airport on time. The Shillong drive takes ~2.5 hrs. Afternoon is packed — pace yourselves.
Day 02

Shillong → Mawphlang → Cherrapunji | Peaks, Forests & Waterfalls

  • Shillong Peak — Highest point in Meghalaya; panoramic views (Keep ID Proof)
  • Elephant Falls — Beautiful 3-tiered waterfall just outside Shillong
  • Laitlum Canyon — The dramatic 'End of Hills' canyon with breathtaking gorge views
  • Mawphlang Sacred Forest — Ancient protected forest with mystical tribal significance
  • Mawkdok Dympep Valley View Point — Stunning suspended bridge viewpoint over the valley
  • Nohkalikai Falls — India's tallest plunge waterfall (340m), best at dusk
🏨 Stay — Cherrapunji
💡 Tip: Long driving day — start early. ID proof mandatory for Shillong Peak. Carry snacks and water.
Day 03

Classic Sohra Waterfall Circuit | Waterfalls & Valley Walks

  • Seven Sisters Falls — Iconic 7-segmented seasonal waterfall (best Jun–Oct)
  • Mawsmai Cave — Illuminated limestone cave, easy and family-friendly walk-through
  • Wei Sawdong — 3-tier waterfall with emerald pools (stairs involved)
  • Dainthlen Falls — Powerful waterfall steeped in local Khasi legend
  • Afternoon free — Valley photography, local café time, or fog-watching (Sohra magic)
🏨 Stay — Cherrapunji
💡 Tip: Seven Sisters is seasonal. Wei Sawdong involves a descent — wear good grip footwear.
Day 04

Nongriat Double Decker Root Bridge | Full-Day Adventure Trek

  • Tyrna Village — Trek starting point; local guides available
  • Double Decker Living Root Bridge — UNESCO-nominated bioengineering marvel
  • Rainbow Falls — Natural rainbow waterfall at the end of the trail (seasonal)
  • Nongriat Village — Warm Khasi hospitality mid-trail
🏨 Stay — Cherrapunji
💡 Tip: 7–8 hour full activity day. Expect 3,500+ steps each way. Carry energy bars, extra water, and wear trekking shoes. Not suitable for people with knee issues.
Day 05

Cherrapunji → Dawki → Mawlynnong | Crystal Rivers & Cleanest Village

  • Umngot River, Dawki — Boating on one of the world's clearest rivers; glass-water effect
  • Mawlynnong — Asia's cleanest village (2003); bamboo walkways & community eco-tourism
  • Riwai Living Root Bridge — Single-decker living root bridge near Mawlynnong
🏨 Stay — Dawki · Betelnut Resort
💡 Tip: Arrive Dawki before 2 PM for the best river clarity and light. Book boating in advance during peak season.
Day 06

Dawki → Guwahati | Departure Day

  • Scenic morning drive through the hills of Meghalaya
  • Transfer to Guwahati Airport for departure (~1 PM)
✈️ Departure — Guwahati
💡 Tip: Allow 4–5 hours for the Dawki–Guwahati drive. Factor in border-area traffic near Dawki.

Before You Go

Travel Tips & Essentials

🌤️ Best Time
October – May. Monsoon (Jun–Sep) is magical but roads can be challenging.
🏨 Stays
2N Shillong ((Super-Deluxe Category Rooms)) · 2N Cherrapunji (Super-Deluxe Category Rooms) · 1N Dawki (Super-Deluxe Category Rooms)
📶 Connectivity
Download offline maps before you go. Signal is patchy in Mawsynram & Nongriat.
📋 Permits
No ILP required for Indian nationals. Foreigners may need a Restricted Area Permit.
🎒 Gear
Waterproof shoes, rain jacket, torch/headlamp, and a refillable water bottle.
💪 Health
Day 4 trek is strenuous. Basic fitness required. Carry ORS and basic first aid.
