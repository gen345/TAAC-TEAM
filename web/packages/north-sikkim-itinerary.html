Your Journey Plan

NJP / IXB → Gangtok → Lachen → Lachung → Gangtok → NJP / IXB

Day 01

NJP / IXB → Gangtok | Arrival · Teesta Valley Drive · MG Marg

  • Pickup from NJP Railway Station / Bagdogra Airport — Begin your North Sikkim journey
  • Scenic drive along the Teesta River — Stunning river gorge and mountain views en route
  • Arrive Gangtok — Check-in at hotel and freshen up
  • Evening free for leisure / MG Marg exploration — Gangtok's vibrant pedestrian boulevard; local food, shopping and city energy
🏨 Stay — Gangtok
💡 Tip: NJP to Gangtok is approx 4–5 hrs on winding mountain roads. Avoid heavy meals before the drive. MG Marg is best explored after 6 PM.
Day 02

Gangtok → Lachen | Mountain Drive · Waterfalls · Chungthang

  • Seven Sisters Waterfall — A stunning cascade of 7 waterfalls visible from the roadside
  • Naga Waterfall — Powerful and scenic waterfall en route to North Sikkim
  • Chungthang — Scenic confluence of Lachen Chu and Lachung Chu rivers; brief stopover
  • Arrive Lachen — Check-in at hotel and overnight rest before the early morning lake visit
🏨 Stay — Lachen
💡 Tip: Roads beyond Chungthang are narrow and high-altitude. Carry warm layers. Early arrival at Lachen recommended for rest before the 4 AM start.
Day 03

Gurudongmar Lake → Lachung | Sacred Lake · High Altitude · Transfer

  • Gurudongmar Lake (17,100 ft) — One of the world's highest lakes; sacred to Buddhists and Sikhs; breathtaking high-altitude landscape
  • Kala Patthar Viewpoint — Stunning panoramic view of surrounding Himalayan peaks (at extra cost)
  • Return to Lachen for breakfast and check-out
  • Transfer to Lachung — Scenic valley road through alpine forests and mountain streams
  • Arrive Lachung — Check-in at hotel and rest
🏨 Stay — Lachung
💡 Tip: Gurudongmar Lake & Kala Patthar are at extra cost. Altitude sickness is possible at 17,100 ft — carry warm clothes, water and medicines. Drive back before noon to avoid cold winds.
Day 04

Yumthang Valley & Zero Point | Valley of Flowers · Zero Point Excursion

  • Yumthang Valley (11,800 ft) — The 'Valley of Flowers of Sikkim'; alpine meadows, hot springs and river views
  • Zero Point / Yumesamdong (15,300 ft) — Last motorable point in North Sikkim; snow-covered landscapes (optional, at extra cost)
  • Return to Lachung — Evening rest and overnight stay
🏨 Stay — Lachung
💡 Tip: Zero Point is optional and charged extra. Carry very warm clothing as temperatures can drop below freezing even in summer. Confirm Zero Point road availability before departure.
Day 05

Lachung → Gangtok | Return Drive · Shopping at MG Marg

  • Drive back to Gangtok — Scenic valley road descent through North Sikkim
  • Arrive Gangtok — Check-in at hotel and freshen up
  • Evening free for shopping at MG Marg — Pick up local handicrafts, woolens and Sikkimese souvenirs
🏨 Stay — Gangtok
💡 Tip: Keep the return drive relaxed and enjoy the mountain scenery. MG Marg evening is a great opportunity for last-minute shopping and local street food.
Day 06

Gangtok → NJP / IXB | Departure Day

  • Check-out from Gangtok — Post-breakfast departure
  • Drive to NJP / Bagdogra — Scenic Teesta Valley descent (~4–5 hrs)
  • Drop at NJP Railway Station / Bagdogra Airport — Tour ends with beautiful memories
✈️ Departure — NJP / IXB
💡 Tip: Plan your onward train or flight with sufficient buffer time. The drive can take longer during peak season.

As Per 2026 Rules

Sikkim Permit Regulations

The Government of Sikkim has updated its entry and permit regulations. Please read carefully before booking.

🇮🇳 Indian Nationals
  • No permit required to enter Sikkim state
  • PAP required for North Sikkim — Lachen, Lachung, Yumthang, Gurudongmar Lake, Zero Point & Thangu
  • PAP obtained in Gangtok via registered tour operator. We handle this for you.
🌍 Foreign Nationals / OCI
  • RAP (Restricted Area Permit) mandatory to enter Sikkim — free of cost
  • PAP additionally required for North Sikkim (Lachung, Yumthang, Lachen). Min group of 2 persons. Must apply via Sikkim-registered travel agent.
  • Gurudongmar Lake & Zero Point — NOT open to foreign nationals (international border proximity)
  • From 2026: All RAP/PAP for foreigners must be applied digitally via e-FRRO portal — physical permits discontinued
📋 Documents & Vehicle Rules
  • Carry valid Govt. ID — Aadhaar, Voter ID or Passport. PAN card not accepted
  • 2 passport-size photographs mandatory for all permit applications
  • Only Sikkim-registered vehicles permitted beyond checkpoints — self-drive from Siliguri/NJP not allowed
  • Children below 5 years not permitted at Gurudongmar Lake & Zero Point (extreme altitude ~5,400m)
  • Permits verified at multiple checkpoints — Rangpo, Chungthang, Lachen & Thangu
Note: Permit rules are subject to change by the Sikkim Government. We stay updated on the latest regulations and handle all permit arrangements for our guests. For official information visit sikkimtourism.gov.in
Before You Go

Travel Tips & Essentials

🌤️ Best Time
March–May (spring blooms) and October–December (clear skies). Nov–Feb may have snow at Zero Point and Yumthang.
📋 Permits
Indian nationals require an Inner Line Permit (ILP) for North Sikkim. Foreigners need a Restricted Area Permit. Arrange in Gangtok.
🏔️ Altitude
Gurudongmar Lake is at 17,100 ft and Zero Point at 15,300 ft. Acclimatize in Gangtok before heading north.
💊 Health
Carry altitude sickness medication (Diamox), ORS, warm layers and a basic first aid kit. Avoid alcohol at high altitude.
💰 Extra Cost
Gurudongmar Lake, Kala Patthar Viewpoint and Zero Point are not included and will be charged extra on actuals.
💵 Cash
Carry sufficient cash from Gangtok. ATMs are unavailable beyond Chungthang.
📶 Connectivity
Mobile signal is very limited beyond Gangtok. Download offline maps and inform family before departure.
