Your Journey Plan

Tinsukia → Tezu → Walong → Dong → Kibithu → Kaho → Wakro → Namsai → Tinsukia

Day 01

Tinsukia → Tezu | Arrival Drive · River & Tea Country

  • Bhupen Hazarika Setu — photo stop at India's longest river bridge (9.15 km) over the Lohit
  • Lohit River Viewpoints — multiple scenic roadside stops along the valley
  • Village Tea Gardens — lush tea garden stretches before Arunachal entry
  • ILP Entry Formalities — Arunachal Pradesh Inner Line Permit check
  • Tezu Town Market Walk — evening explore of the local market
  • Lohit River Sunset Point — golden hour by the river in Tezu
🏨 Stay — Tezu
💡 Tip: Long driving day — start by 6–7 AM. Carry snacks and water. Bhupen Setu photo stop is a must.
Day 02

Tezu → Walong | High Mountain Roads · War Memorial

  • Parasuram Kund Road Viewpoint — panoramic view en route towards Walong
  • Waterfall Stretches — multiple cascading falls along the Lohit valley road
  • Mountain Zig-Zag Viewpoint — dramatic switchback views before Walong
  • Riverside Halt — scenic stop near Walong entry point
  • Walong War Memorial — tribute to the 1962 India-China war heroes
  • Lohit Riverside Walk — peaceful evening stroll by the river
🏨 Stay — Walong
💡 Tip: One of the most scenic drives in Northeast India. Roads are narrow — drive cautiously. War Memorial is deeply moving — don't skip it.
Day 03

Dong Valley → Kibithu → Kaho | First Sunrise · Easternmost Frontier

  • Dong Valley Sunrise Trek — witness the first sunrise in India (3:30–4:00 AM start; carry torch & water)
  • Hanging Bridge near Dong Route — thrilling suspension bridge over mountain stream
  • Kibithu — India-China border region; army-permitted scenic zone
  • Kaho Village — easternmost inhabited village of India; unique frontier experience
  • Border Mountain Panoramic Viewpoint — sweeping views of the Indo-China border range
  • Return to Walong by evening
🏨 Stay — Walong
💡 Tip: 4 AM start is non-negotiable for the Dong sunrise. Carry a torch, warm layers, and water. Kibithu & Kaho require ILP — ensure permits are in order the night before.
Day 04

Walong → Wakro | Valley Descent · Ridge & Waterfall Drive

  • Lohit Valley Open Landscape — wide valley views as you descend from the high ranges
  • Waterfall Stretch Midway — natural roadside cascades along the descent
  • Hill Ridge Viewpoint Before Wakro — panoramic ridgeline stop before town
  • Wakro Local Exploration — optional short walk near Wakro hills
🏨 Stay — Wakro
💡 Tip: Manageable drive after the intense Day 3. Use the afternoon to rest or do a light walk. Road conditions improve as you descend.
Day 05

Wakro → Namsai → Tinsukia | Golden Pagoda · Tea Gardens · Farewell

  • Morning countryside drive — peaceful Arunachal–Assam border landscape
  • Golden Pagoda, Namsai (Kongmu Kham Monastery) — one of India's most stunning Theravada Buddhist monasteries
  • Tea Garden Stretch — rolling green tea estates before crossing into Assam
  • Dibru-Saikhowa National Park — brief scenic drive past (subject to time)
  • Drop at Tinsukia railway station or hotel
✈️ Departure — Tinsukia
💡 Tip: Golden Pagoda in Namsai is a highlight — allow 45–60 mins. Plan to reach Tinsukia well before your onward train or flight.

Before You Go

Travel Tips & Essentials

🌤️ Best Season
October–April. Avoid monsoon (May–Sep) — roads can be severely damaged by landslides.
📋 Permits
ILP (Inner Line Permit) mandatory for all non-Arunachal residents. Arrange before travel from Dibrugarh or Tinsukia.
🚙 Vehicle
SUV strongly recommended (Innova Crysta / Scorpio). Roads are rough and narrow beyond Tezu.
🌄 Dong Trek
Moderate difficulty. Carry torch, warm layers, water & energy snacks. Start no later than 3:30 AM.
📶 Connectivity
Very limited or no mobile network beyond Tezu. Download offline maps before departure (Maps.me / OsmAnd).
💵 Cash
Carry sufficient cash from Tinsukia or Dibrugarh. No ATMs beyond Tezu.
