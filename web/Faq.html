Booking a Tour

How it all works from enquiry to confirmation

How do I book a tour with The Adventure Awaits Co.?+
It's simple! You can reach us via WhatsApp, email, or through our website booking form. Share your preferred dates, destination, and group size — we'll send you a detailed itinerary and quote within 24 hours. Once you're happy, pay the advance amount and we'll send you a written confirmation. That's it — you're booked! 🎉
How far in advance should I book?+
We recommend booking at least 4–6 weeks in advance for most destinations. For peak season travel (Durga Puja, December–January, summer holidays), book 2–3 months early — hotels and permits fill up fast, especially for North Sikkim and Meghalaya.
💡 Pro tip: Booking early also locks in the current price — no surprise hikes later!
Can I customise a tour package?+
Absolutely! Customised tours are our speciality. Tell us your budget, dates, interests (adventure / honeymoon / family / sightseeing), and we'll build an itinerary just for you. We love tailoring trips — no two packages need to be the same.
What is the minimum group size for a tour?+
We can arrange tours for individuals, couples, and groups. For fixed departure packages, a minimum of 6–8 travellers is usually required. For private/customised tours, even 2 people is perfectly fine — it's just you and your travel party.
Do you arrange solo travel?+
Yes! We do arrange solo tours. Solo travellers can join our fixed departure group tours (great for meeting fellow travellers!) or book a private customised itinerary. We'll make sure you feel safe and well taken care of throughout the trip.
Can families with young children travel on your tours?+
Of course! We love family trips. We'll suggest kid-friendly itineraries, comfortable stays, and avoid strenuous activities unless you want them. Just let us know the ages of your children when enquiring so we can plan accordingly.

Payments & Pricing

How much, when, and how to pay

How much advance do I need to pay to confirm a booking?+
We require a 25%–50% advance (token amount) of the total package cost to confirm your booking. The exact percentage is mentioned in your quotation. The remaining balance must be paid at least 15 days before your departure.
What payment methods do you accept?+
We accept:
  • UPI (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm, etc.)
  • NEFT / IMPS bank transfer
  • Cash (at our discretion — please confirm in advance)
All payments must be in Indian Rupees (INR). After paying, please share the payment screenshot or UTR number on WhatsApp for quick confirmation.
Is GST included in the package price?+
GST is not included in quoted prices unless explicitly stated as "GST Inclusive" in your itinerary. Applicable GST will be mentioned separately in the final invoice. This is standard practice for Indian tour operators.
Can I pay in instalments / EMI?+
We don't directly offer an EMI scheme, but we're flexible! If you need to split the balance into 2–3 instalments (before the full payment due date), just talk to us — we'll work something out. We'd rather find a solution than lose a good traveller! 😊
Are there any hidden charges I should know about?+
We're completely transparent about pricing. Things not included in the standard package price are:
  • Train / flight tickets to the departure city
  • Entry fees to parks, monuments, or restricted areas
  • Personal expenses (tips, laundry, room service, alcohol)
  • Travel insurance
  • Any meals not listed in inclusions
These will always be mentioned clearly in your itinerary document.

Tour Packages

What's included and what to expect

What does MAP (Meal Plan) mean in your packages?+
MAP stands for Modified American Plan — it includes breakfast and dinner at your hotel/homestay. Lunch is generally on your own (roadside dhabas and local restaurants are part of the experience!). If a package includes all three meals, it will be clearly mentioned as Full Board.
What type of accommodation do you provide?+
Depending on the package and destination, we arrange hotels, homestays, guesthouses, and tented camps. Budget packages use clean, comfortable local guesthouses and homestays. Standard and premium packages use proper hotels with attached bathrooms. All accommodation details are listed in your itinerary.
What kind of vehicle will be used for the tour?+
For small groups (2–6 pax) we typically use Innova Crysta, Xylo, or similar SUVs. Larger groups travel in Tempo Travellers or minibuses. All vehicles are reserved exclusively for your group — no shared cabs unless specifically mentioned.
Will there be a tour guide with us?+
Local guides are included for destinations where they add real value (e.g., Meghalaya forest treks, Kaziranga safari, historical sites). For standard road trips, your experienced local driver often doubles as your guide. If you'd like a professional guide throughout, let us know at the time of booking — we can add it to your package.
Can I change the hotel category after booking?+
Yes, upgrades or changes to hotel category are possible subject to availability. The price difference will be adjusted accordingly. Please request changes in writing (WhatsApp/email) as early as possible. An amendment fee of ₹500 per person may apply for changes after confirmation.

Destinations

Best seasons, permits, and destination-specific info

What is the best time to visit Northeast India (Meghalaya / Sikkim)?+
  • Meghalaya: October–May (avoid monsoon June–Sept — it's the wettest place on Earth!)
  • Sikkim: March–May (spring, rhododendrons) and October–December (clear skies, Kanchenjunga views)
  • North Bengal / Darjeeling: March–May and October–November
💡 Note: Monsoon has its magic too — Meghalaya's waterfalls are breathtaking. Just be prepared for road closures and landslides.
Do I need a special permit for Sikkim?+
Yes! North Sikkim requires a Protected Area Permit (PAP), which we arrange on your behalf — just send us your Aadhaar/Voter ID at least 10 days before departure. For East Sikkim (Gangtok area), no special permit is needed for Indian nationals. Foreign nationals require additional permits — please inform us in advance.
Do I need a permit for the Andaman Islands?+
Indian nationals do not need a permit for Port Blair, Havelock (Swaraj Dweep), or Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep). However, certain restricted areas (Barren Island, tribal areas) require special permits. If your itinerary includes any restricted zones, we'll handle the permit process for you.
What is the best time for Kashmir?+
  • Spring (March–May): Tulip gardens, mild weather — most popular
  • Summer (June–August): Cool escape from the heat, Gulmarg & Pahalgam at their best
  • Autumn (Sept–Nov): Chinar trees turning golden — absolutely magical
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Snow, skiing at Gulmarg — for the brave!
Is it safe to travel to these destinations right now?+
All our destinations — Northeast India, Kashmir, and Andaman — are popular, well-visited tourist areas and generally very safe for travellers. We monitor local conditions closely and will proactively inform you of any advisories. Feel free to ask us directly before booking.

Safety & Health

Staying safe on the road and in the mountains

Is travel insurance compulsory?+
It's not mandatory, but we strongly recommend it — especially for high-altitude destinations like North Sikkim or adventure activities. A good travel insurance plan covers medical emergencies, trip cancellations, baggage loss, and emergency evacuation. We don't sell insurance ourselves, but we're happy to guide you on what to look for.
What about altitude sickness for North Sikkim or high-altitude destinations?+
Altitude sickness (AMS) is a real concern for Lachen, Lachung, Gurudongmar Lake, and similar high-altitude spots. We recommend:
  • Consulting your doctor before the trip
  • Carrying Diamox (acetazolamide) — consult your doctor first
  • Staying hydrated, avoiding alcohol the first 2 days
  • Acclimatising properly — don't rush the ascent
If you have heart conditions, respiratory issues, or are pregnant, please consult your doctor before booking high-altitude tours.
Are the tours suitable for senior travellers?+
Yes! We have many senior travellers with us and love planning comfortable, manageable itineraries for them. Just let us know when booking — we'll choose flatter routes, comfortable hotels, avoid strenuous treks, and ensure the pace is relaxed. Health and comfort come first.
What happens if someone falls ill during the tour?+
Your safety is our first priority. In case of illness, our local driver/guide will assist in reaching the nearest medical facility immediately. We have contacts at local hospitals and clinics in all our destination areas. This is another reason we strongly recommend travel insurance for medical cover and evacuation if needed.

Documents & ID

What to carry and what we need from you

What ID documents do I need to carry on the tour?+
Every traveller must carry a valid government-issued photo ID. Accepted IDs include:
  • Aadhaar Card (most commonly accepted)
  • Voter ID Card
  • Passport
  • Driving Licence (with photo)
⚠️ Note: PAN Card is NOT accepted at permit checkpoints (North Sikkim, etc.). Always carry Aadhaar or Voter ID.
We also recommend carrying 2 passport-size photos and photocopies of your ID for permits.
What documents do I need to send you before the tour?+
For most tours, we'll need a clear photo/scan of your Aadhaar or Voter ID for hotel pre-registration and permit applications. For destinations requiring permits (North Sikkim, etc.), please send documents at least 10 days before departure so we have enough time to process them.
Can foreign nationals / NRIs travel on your packages?+
Yes! We welcome travellers of all nationalities. Foreign nationals and OCI cardholders should carry their Passport and valid Indian visa / OCI card. Some destinations (Sikkim, Andaman restricted areas) require additional permits for foreign nationals — please inform us when booking so we can arrange everything in time.

Cancellations & Changes

What happens if plans change?

How do I cancel my booking?+
All cancellation requests must be made in writing — via email or WhatsApp to our team. The date we receive your written request is treated as the cancellation date. Verbal cancellations are not accepted. See our full Cancellation Policy for the charge slabs.
Can I reschedule instead of cancelling?+
Yes — and we always prefer rescheduling over cancellation! If you need to change dates, let us know as early as possible. Rescheduling is subject to availability and any price difference (e.g., peak season vs off-season) will be adjusted. An amendment fee of ₹500 per person may apply.
What if the company cancels the tour?+
In the rare case that we need to cancel (natural calamity, insufficient group size, safety concerns), we'll offer you either a full credit note valid for 12 months OR a refund of the amount paid minus non-recoverable costs. We'll always inform you as early as possible and work with you to find the best alternative.
How long does a refund take?+
Eligible refunds are processed within 7–15 working days after approval. Refunds are made to the original payment source (same bank account / UPI). See our full Refund Policy for complete details.
